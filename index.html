Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post‑doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. Here’s a summary of my favourites, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance
A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300 BC, when a whole village danced around in a circle like chickens to encourage their livestock to be “fruitful”.
Icelandic brownian shuffle
Before Icelanders developed fire, they practiced this dance—huddling in a circle and shuffling tiny, rapid movements. One student thought it inspired modern styles such as Twerking.
Arctic robot dance
An interesting example of historic misinformation: English explorers in the 1960s thought they’d discovered a robotic-style dance practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska—but they were simply freezing.
